RCEM Quality Improvement Guide and QIAT 2025

The Royal College of Emergency Medicine requires you to participate in quality improvement throughout your training. Specialty Learning Outcome 11 mandates continuous assessment of your skills in patient safety and service improvement.

The Quality Improvement Assessment Tool (QIAT)

You must complete a QIAT form annually on the Kaizen ePortfolio. Your Educational Supervisor reviews and signs this off before your Annual Review of Competence Progression. The 2025 curriculum update moves away from traditional audits. You must demonstrate mastery of systematic improvement methodologies.

Quality Improvement Methodology and Theory

The RCEM recommends the Model for Improvement. This model asks three fundamental questions.

  • What are we trying to accomplish? This is your SMART aim.
  • How will we know that a change is an improvement? These are your outcome, process, and balance measures.
  • What change can we make that will result in improvement? These are your specific change ideas.

You test these change ideas using Plan, Do, Study, Act cycles. You plan the test, carry it out, study the results using run charts, and act on what you learn. You should run multiple, small cycles rather than one large intervention.

Example Case: Asthma Discharge Standards

A Higher Specialist Trainee noticed poor compliance with RCEM asthma discharge standards. They reviewed the data and found patients lacked documented peak flows and showed poor inhaler technique.

  • Aim: 80 percent of asthmatic patients discharged from the emergency department will meet RCEM standards by July 2025.
  • Methodology: The trainee used driver diagrams to identify root causes. They implemented changes over six Plan, Do, Study, Act cycles.
  • Changes: Initial cycles focused on staff reminders and educational updates. Later cycles focused on improving digital documentation templates.
  • Results: Continuous data collection showed sustained improvement on a statistical process control chart. The trainee involved nurses, junior doctors, and consultants to ensure the changes remained sustainable.

Progressive Competency Requirements

  • Core Trainees: You must participate effectively in a departmental project and understand basic improvement principles.
  • Intermediate Trainees: You must progress to independent data analysis, evaluate changes, and reflect on your project outcomes.
  • Higher Trainees: You must provide clinical leadership, drive effective change, and support a culture of good clinical governance within the department.
